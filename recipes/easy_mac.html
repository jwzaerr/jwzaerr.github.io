Easy, Mac.

This recipe uses a roux that was first taught to me by my Grandmother and one my Father still uses today to make Biscuits and Gravy. The ratio is simple and scalable. Add different fats like bacon or sausage for a good breakfast gravy or use butter and later add cheese for a quick cheese sauce.

Ingredients

Yields one serving. Double, triple or Quadruple as necessary.

  • 4 oz of dried pasta
  • 1 T butter
  • 1 T All purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 oz grated parmesan cheese (or more!) You can use cheddar or a mix of both or whatever you have that sounds good.
  • Lots of pepper
  • Salt to taste, check before adding any. Youy cheese may be salty enough.

Method

Start heating up a pot of water for the pasta. Melt butter in a sauce pan and add the flour to make a rough paste. Whisk milk into the paste. Once the mixture is cohesive, bring to a slight bubbling before you start adding the cheese a little at a time. Add pepper and salt to taste.

End the boiling noodles a minute or two before al dente and add them to the cheese sauce pot. Finish the noodles over low heat in the cheese sauce until al dente.
