Banana Bread

A humble plot original! Whenever we have bananas that have a rough go on a bike ride or just get too ripe, we stick them in the freezer to use later for this recipe. When you pull the bananas out to thaw it'd be a good time to pull that stick of butter out of the koolerator to soften too.

Ingredients

Yields one loaf

  • Wet ingredients

  • (4oz.) One stick of butter, softened
  • (165g) 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2-3 very ripe bananas
  • 1 t vanilla

  • Dry ingredients

  • (125g) 1 cup All purpose flour
  • (~50g) 1/2 cup Rolled oats
  • (65g) 1/2 cup Whole wheat flour
  • (8g) 1 t kosher salt
  • (7 1/2 g) 1 1/2 t Baking Powder

  • Options

  • 1/2 cup nuts
  • 1/2 cup shredded coconut
  • Chocolate to taste

Method

Mix dry ingredients. Mix wet ingredients with a hand or a stand mixer....or do yourself a favor and whisk it by hand.

Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients and mix until combined.

Butter up a bread loaf pan. I prefer pyrex over metal for this bread. I find the metal gives me too crispy of a crust, but I can see wanting that, just watch your bake time at the end to not over do it.

Bake in a 350 degree oven for 45-60 minutes or when a toothpick comes out clean from the top middle center.

Cool in pan for about five minutes. I like to take a hamburger flipper and run it down the sides of the pan, ever so slightly scooping to make sure the bottom releases and it all comes out clean.

Leave it to cool on the rack until you just cant take it anymore and eat some. I usually make this at night and leave a cotton towel over it while it cools on the rack and eat it in the morning.
